Qiyas - Exam Center

Responsibilities

Designed and implemented a comprehensive computer vision system for real-time student behavior analysis across 20 surveillance cameras. The project involved integrating trained keypoint detection models into a modular pipeline, conducting detailed model evaluations (MMPOSE vs. YOLO Pose), and optimizing for minimal false positives.

Led data collection and annotation workflows, trained and validated multiple models with varying configurations, and developed robust logic-based and ML-based algorithms to detect specific actions such as looking around, hand raise, and hand in pocket. Approaches included geometric rule-based methods, Random Forests, Decision Trees, shallow neural networks, and hybrid deep learning models, alongside advanced preprocessing techniques like keypoint normalization and ROI-based adjustment.

Manually curated datasets with over 63,000 annotated rows, tested diverse model architectures and feature engineering methods (including temporal vector integration), and continuously improved system performance. The system was scaled from a single- camera setup to a full-scale deployment across 20 cameras, with each camera calibrated for customized ROI and detection tuning.

Successfully deployed the solution to a production server, where it is now actively monitoring and detecting student actions in a live center environment with up to 95% accuracy.
